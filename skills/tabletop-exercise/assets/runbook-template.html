    ✓ DECISION POINT

    Q1.1 — Containment vs. preservation. The on-call wants to kill -9 the suspicious processes immediately. The Tech Lead wants forensic snapshots first. You have 90 seconds before the next set of hosts may be affected.

    Owner: Incident Commander.

    Capture: What did you decide and why? Log it.

    ▸ BRANCH

    The Q1.1 decision shapes Phase 2:

    • If "kill processes immediately": Containment is fast but evidence is partly destroyed. Phase 2 begins with the forensics team unable to determine the initial vector. Facilitator drops Inject 2-A: missing logs.
    • If "snapshot first, then contain": 4-minute delay; one additional host is hit. Phase 2 begins with full forensic data. Facilitator drops Inject 2-B: clean evidence chain.
    • If team can't reach a decision in 90s: Facilitator role-plays the on-call making a unilateral call. Note this as a command-and-control gap for the AAR.
